This thesis contributes to the field of social media recommendation systems by proposing a user-centered and privacy-preserving recommendation framework implemented through a Chrome extension. Unlike traditional recommendation approaches that rely mainly on implicit behavioral signals, the proposed system allows users to explicitly label social media posts as “Recommended” or “Unwanted,” thereby creating a clearer and more transparent preference model. Another contribution of this work is the integration of lightweight local personalization through techniques such as Low-Rank Adaptation (LoRA), which enables efficient model adaptation on the user’s device without requiring extensive centralized data collection. In addition, the thesis introduces a cross-platform browser-based architecture that operates on publicly visible content, making the approach more flexible and independent from private platform APIs. Overall, this work contributes a practical, transparent, and ethical alternative to conventional opaque recommendation systems by combining explicit feedback, local learning, and cross-platform usability.
